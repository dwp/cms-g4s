Your options: Use the Child Maintenance Service

{% if data['will-you-be-paying-or-receiving'] === 'paying' %}

The Child Maintenance Service may be able to arrange child maintenance for you. This is less flexible than making your own arrangement because the calculation is a fixed amount based on your earnings.

Using the Child Maintenance Service might be a good option for if you do not feel safe dealing with the other parent, or you do not think they will stick to an agreement.

The Child Maintenance Service will:

  • work out how much child maintenance you will pay
  • arrange for the you to pay the other parent
  • take action if you do not make payments
  • help find the other parent if you’ve lost contact
  • recalculate the payment amount if a changes in your circumstances is reported
  • help if someone denies they’re the parent of a child

There are 2 ways you can make child maintenance payments

Direct Pay

You pay maintenance directly into the other parent’s bank account.

You do not need to have any contact with the other parent and there are no additional fees.

Collect and Pay

The Child Maintenance Service will collect maintenance you and pass it to the other parent.

There are additional fees for you and the other parent and you can only use Collect and Pay if the other parent agrees to it.
