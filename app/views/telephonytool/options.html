Options

I’m going to tell you about your child maintenance options now. Feel free to stop me at any point to ask questions or if anything is unclear.

There are 2 options available to you. You can make your own child maintenance arrangement, or you can use the Child Maintenance Service.

If the user has experienced any domestic abuse or has a poor relationship with the other parent

I know that making your own arrangement might not be the best option for you, but I still need to give you all the information so you know what options are available and then you can make an informed decision about what you want to do.

If the user has a preference for one option

I know you mentioned that you want to [use the Child Maintenance Service/ set up your own arrangement], but I’ll go through both options with you anyway so that you have all the information and then you can make a decision about what you want to do

About making your own arrangement:

You can choose to make your own arrangement. This will be a private agreement between you and the other parent. No one else will be involved and it can be whatever you both agree to.

Some parents prefer to make their own arrangement because:

  • you can come to an agreement with the other parent about how much payments should be
  • it can be totally flexible, so you can change the amount if you need to
  • you can decide how often payments will be made
  • You can agree payments for specific things, like school uniform or trips

This type of arrangement usually works best with parents who feel safe dealing with each other and who think they can both stick to whatever is agreed.

Do you have any questions about making your own arrangement, before I tell you more about using the Child Maintenance Service?

About using the Child Maintenance Service

Using the Child Maintenance Service is less flexible than making your own arrangement because the amount of money you [get/ pay] is fixed based on [your/ the other parents] earnings.

If no DA / Under 19
There is a £20 fee to make an application. This is non-refundable and unfortunately, we cannot always guarantee an arrangement.

If they ask why?
Because we cannot always find the other parent, but the more information you can tell us about them if you choose to apply, the more likely we’ll be able to set something up for you.

Using the Child Maintenance Service might be a good option if you don’t want to have any contact with the other parent. If you choose to use Child Maintenance Service, you’ll need to complete an application online or on the phone then the Child Maintenance Service will work out how much maintenance you should [get/ pay] and arrange for payments to be made,

About payment types and fees

If you decide to use the Child Maintenance Service, there are two ways [you/ the other parent] can make payments.

Direct Pay
is where [you pay / the other parent pays] maintenance direct to [your/ their] bank account. You do not need to have any contact with the other parent and the Child Maintenance Service can take action if payments are not made. Either parent can choose to use Direct Pay and there are no additional fees to use it.

Collect and Pay
is where we collect money from [you/ the other parent] and pass it on to [you/ the other parent]. There are additional fees for both parents if you use this service and you can only start with this type of arrangement if [you choose to/ the other parent agrees to it].

Most parents who use the Child Maintenance Service will start with Direct Pay. However, we can enforce Collect and Pay if [you/ the other parent] stop making direct payments or do not respond to us.
