Your child maintenance options

If the caller:

  • has experienced domestic abuse
  • is concerned about contacting the other parent
  • would prefer one option

You still need to go through both options to make sure they have all the information before they make a decision.

Making your own arrangement:

  • this is a private arrangement between you and the other parent
  • no one else will be involved
  • it can be whatever you and the other parent agree to

You and the other parent can:

  • come to an agreement about how much payments should be
  • change the amount if you need to
  • decide how often payments will be made
  • agree payments for specific things, like school uniform or trips

Making your own arrangement might be a good option if:

  • you feel safe dealing with the other parent
  • you think the other parent will stick to an agreement

Do you have any questions about making your own arrangement?

Using the Child Maintenance Service

  • the Child Maintenance Service is for parents who cannot agree to a private arrangement
  • it is less flexible than making your own arrangement because the amount is based on the paying parent's earnings
  • it might be a good option if you do not feel safe with the other parent or if you have not been able to stick to your own arrangement in the past

Application fee

  • there is a £20 fee to make an application
  • the fee is non-refundable
  • it does not guarantee an arrangement

You need to complete an application online or on the phone. Then the Child Maintenance Service will:

  • work out child maintenance payment amount
  • arrange for payments to be made between the parents
  • take action if payments are not made
  • help find the other parent if you've lost contact
  • recalculate the payment amount if changes in parents' circumstances are reported
  • help if someone denies they're the parent of a child

How you receive child maintenance payments

Direct Pay

  • the other parent will pay maintenance directly to your bank account
  • you do not need to have any contact with the other parent
  • there are no additional fees

Collect and Pay

  • the child maintenance service will collect maintenance from the other parent and pass it on to you
  • there are additional fees for you and the other parent
  • you can only start with Collect & Pay if the other parent agrees to it or if they do not respond to us
