Your options

Make your own arrangement

You can make your own maintenance arrangements between parents. No one else has to be involved and it can be whatever you agree. For example, you and the other parent can:

  • decide how much you will pay or receive
  • change the amount when needed
  • decide how often payments will be made
  • agree for a parent to pay for specific things, like school uniforms or trips

Making your own arrangement might be a good option if:

  • you feel safe dealing with the other parent
  • you think the other parent will stick to an agreement

Use the Child Maintenance Service

The Child Maintenance Service may be able to arrange child maintenance for you. This is less flexible than making your own arrangement because the calculation is a fixed amount based on the paying parent’s earnings.

The Child Maintenance Service will:

  • work out child maintenance payment amounts
  • arrange for payments to be made between the parents
  • take action if payments are not made
  • help find the other parent if you've lost contact
  • recalculate the payment amount if changes in parents’ circumstances are reported
  • help if someone denies they’re the parent of a child
