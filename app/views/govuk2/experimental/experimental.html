Making a child maintenance
arrangement

Overview

Child Maintenance is paid from one parent to another to contribute towards the cost of raising their child. The parent who has main custody of their child is entitled to receive payments.

You can arrange child maintenance yourself. This is the most flexible arrangement you can have, because parents work together to:

  • decide how much to pay
  • choose how often payments will be made
  • talk about things other than payments – such as paying for school uniforms or trips

You can also use the Child Maintenance Service. This is less flexible but may be a better option if you are not comfortable dealing with the other parent, or you don’t think they will pay.

You don’t get to choose how much is paid if you use the Child Maintenance Service. They’ll work this out based on the circumstances of both parents.

If you've experienced domestic abuse or controlling behaviour

Domestic abuse can include, but is not limited to:

  • psychological or emotional abuse
  • physical or sexual abuse
  • financial or economic abuse
  • coercive control (an act or pattern of acts of assault, threats, humiliation, intimidation, degradation, isolation and control)
  • harassment and stalking
  • online or digital abuse

You can use the Child Maintenance Service to arrange child maintenance if you do not want to contact the other parent yourself. They will contact the other parent for you.

You can get help and support if you've experienced domestic abuse.

Get help to make your own arrangement

You can use the child maintenance calculator to help you decide how much payments should be. This will show you how much would be paid if you used the Child Maintenance Service.

You can also find help with:

  • working out the cost of raising your children
  • having a conversation about child maintenance
  • recording your agreement

You can contact Child Maintenance Options for advice.

Use the Child Maintenance Service

You can use the Child Maintenance Service to arrange child maintenance. It’s less flexible than making your own arrangement because they will decide how much is paid, and when payments happen.

The Child Maintenance Service will:

  • work out child maintenance payment amounts
  • arrange for the other parent to pay
  • take action if payments are not made
  • help find the other parent if you've lost contact
  • look at the payments if changes in parents’ circumstances are reported
  • help if someone denies they’re the parent of a child

Eligibility

To be eligible to use the Child Maintenance Service your child needs to be under 16, or under 20 if they’re in full-time education.

You can apply if you’re:

  • either parent
  • a guardian of the child
  • a child over 12 living in Scotland

If one parent is in prison or a full-time student with no income then they will not have to pay child maintenance.

If a parent lives outside the UK

You cannot apply if the parent with main day-to-day care lives outside the UK.

The service can only help if the paying parent works outside the UK for a British organisation.

Fees

The application fee is £20. You will not have to pay this if you:

  • have experienced domestic abuse
  • are under 19 years old
  • live in Northern Ireland

Usually one parent will pay the other direct. There is no fee for this.

If this is not possible for the Child Maintenance Service can arrange to collect the money from the paying parent.

There is an extra fee for this service, which is:

  • 20% (which is added to the payment) for paying parents
  • 4% (which is taken off the payment) for receiving parents

How to apply

Contact Child Maintenance Options before you apply.

They’ll discuss your maintenance arrangements with you and explain how to apply.

You’ll need to give information about you and your family, for example:

  • your National Insurance number
  • details about the child you’re applying for
  • details about the other parent and your relationship with them
